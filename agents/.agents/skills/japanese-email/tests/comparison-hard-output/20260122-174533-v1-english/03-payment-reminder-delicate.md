# Test: 03-payment-reminder-delicate (V1 - English)

**Prompt:**
```
Write a Japanese business email to a long-term client whose payment is 30 days overdue. This is your second reminder. You must be firm but preserve the 10-year business relationship. The amount is ¥500,000.
```

**Response:**

I'll write a professional Japanese business email for this second payment reminder. Given the 10-year relationship and the delicate nature of overdue payment collection, I need to balance firmness with respect using appropriate keigo.

---

〇〇株式会社

お支払期日より30日が経過いたしましたが、本日現在、ご入金の確認が取れておりません。弊社といたしましても、貴社との10年にわたるお取引を大変重要なものと考えており、今後とも良好な関係を継続させていただきたく存じております。

**Key elements of this email:**

1. **Seasonal greeting omitted** - For payment reminders, seasonal greetings are typically omitted as they can seem inconsistent with the serious nature of the request

2. **Teineigo/Kenjogo balance** - Uses humble forms (申し上げます、存じております) while maintaining firm but respectful tone

3. **Relationship acknowledgment** - Explicitly mentions the 10-year relationship (「10年にわたるお取引」) to show you value the partnership

4. **Clear action request** - Specifies exact amount and requests a new deadline

5. **Face-saving clause** - Includes "行き違い" (行き違いにて失礼いたしました) - acknowledges they may have already paid

6. **Door left open** - Invites them to communicate if there are circumstances (「ご事情がございましたら」) - maintains relationship

7. **Firmness through structure** - Using 「記」format with bullet points makes the request unambiguous and professional
